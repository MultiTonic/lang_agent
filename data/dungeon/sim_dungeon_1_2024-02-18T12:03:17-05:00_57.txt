```makefile
<dungeon> ::= <path> | <room> | <treasure> | <monster>

<action> ::= "and encounter" | "."
<room> ::= <description> "In this room," <event>
<event> ::= "you find" | "there is"
<treasure> ::= <description> "Hidden in a corner, you find a" <type_name>
<monster> ::= <description> "A ferocious" <type_name> "jumps out at you!"
<description> ::= <adjective> <noun>
<adjective> ::= "dark" | "dank" | "musty" | "cold" | "slimy"
<noun> ::= "corridor" | "room" | "passageway" | "chamber" | "cavern"
<type_name> ::= <simple_type> | <complex_type>
<simple_type> ::= "rat" | "goblin" | "troll" | "skeleton" | "zombie"
<complex_type> ::= <simple_type> "with a" <special_quality>
<special_quality> ::= <material> | <color> | <shape> | <ability>
<material> ::= "of adamantium" | "made of mithril" | "forged from iron"
<color> ::= "red" | "green" | "blue" | "black" | "white"
<shape> ::= "with three heads" | "twisted into a knot" | "wrapped in chains"
<ability> ::= "that can fly" | "that breathes fire" | "that is invisible"
```

A `<type_name>` can be either a simple type such as "rat" or "goblin", or a complex type that includes an additional special quality. A `<special_quality>` can be a material, color, shape, or ability, each of which adds more detail to the type name.
